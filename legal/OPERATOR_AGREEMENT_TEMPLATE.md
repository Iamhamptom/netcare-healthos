## 1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 In this Agreement, unless the context indicates otherwise:

**"Agreement"** means this Data Processing and Service Agreement, including all Schedules;

**"CPA"** means the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008;

**"Data Subject"** means the person to whom the Personal Information relates, including patients, practitioners, and practice staff;

**"ECTA"** means the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002;

**"Effective Date"** means the date on which the Practice accepts this Agreement and completes the first subscription payment;

**"Health Information"** means any Personal Information concerning the physical or mental health of a Data Subject, including their medical history, as defined in section 26 of POPIA and the Health Information Regulations;

**"Health Information Regulations"** means the Regulations Relating to the Processing of Data Subjects' Health Information, 2026 (Government Gazette No. 54268, Notice No. 7198, effective 6 March 2026);

**"HPCSA"** means the Health Professions Council of South Africa;

**"Information Officer"** means the person registered with the Information Regulator in terms of section 55 of POPIA;

**"Information Regulator"** means the Information Regulator established under section 39 of POPIA;

**"Operator"** means VisioCorp (Pty) Ltd, which processes Personal Information on behalf of the Responsible Party;

**"Personal Information"** has the meaning ascribed to it in section 1 of POPIA, and includes information relating to an identifiable, living, natural person and, where applicable, an identifiable, existing juristic person;

**"Platform"** means the Netcare Health OS software-as-a-service application accessible at https://healthos.visiocorp.co and related APIs;

**"POPIA"** means the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013;

**"Responsible Party"** means the Practice, which determines the purpose of and means for processing Personal Information;

**"Security Compromise"** has the meaning ascribed in section 22 of POPIA — any event where there are reasonable grounds to believe that Personal Information has been accessed or acquired by any unauthorised person;

**"Special Personal Information"** means information as defined in section 26 of POPIA, including information concerning a Data Subject's health, sex life, religious or philosophical beliefs, race or ethnic origin, trade union membership, political persuasion, biometric information, or criminal behaviour;

**"Sub-Processor"** means any third party engaged by the Operator to process Personal Information on behalf of the Responsible Party.

## 2. SCOPE OF PROCESSING

2.1 The Responsible Party engages the Operator to provide the Platform, which includes AI-powered healthcare practice management tools, and in so doing, to process Personal Information on behalf of the Responsible Party.

2.2 The nature, purpose, categories of Data Subjects, and types of Personal Information processed are detailed in **Schedule A**.

2.3 The Operator shall process Personal Information solely for the purpose of providing the Platform and related services to the Responsible Party, and for no other purpose.

2.4 The Operator does not acquire any ownership rights or independent rights of use in respect of the Personal Information processed under this Agreement. All Personal Information remains the property of the Responsible Party.

2.5 The Operator's access to the Practice's data is limited to what is necessary for providing the Platform services. The Operator does not access, connect to, or modify the Practice's existing on-premise systems, electronic health records, or practice management software unless explicitly authorised in writing.

## 3. TERM AND SUBSCRIPTION

3.1 **Initial Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue on a month-to-month basis in accordance with the subscription tier selected by the Responsible Party (see **Schedule D**).

3.2 **Renewal.** The subscription renews automatically each month unless cancelled by either Party with not less than 30 days' written notice.

3.3 **Payment.** Subscription fees are payable monthly in advance via the Yoco payment platform. Fees are debited on the same date each month as the initial subscription payment.

3.4 **Price Changes.** The Operator shall provide not less than 60 days' written notice of any change to subscription fees. The Responsible Party may terminate within 30 days of receiving such notice without penalty.

3.5 **Suspension.** The Operator may suspend access to the Platform if subscription fees remain unpaid for more than 14 days, provided the Operator has given 7 days' written notice of the overdue amount. During suspension, all Personal Information shall remain securely stored and accessible upon payment of arrears.

5.2 The Operator shall process Health Information only under a duty of confidentiality as contemplated in section 32(2) of POPIA, whether arising from:

- (b) a professional code of conduct;
- (c) an employment relationship; or

- (a) physical and electronic records protection;

The current authorised transfer is detailed in clause 9.

5.6 The Responsible Party warrants that it has a lawful basis under section 27 read with section 32 of POPIA for processing the Health Information of its patients, and that it has obtained any necessary authorisations or consents.

## 7. SECURITY COMPROMISE NOTIFICATION

7.1 **Operator to Responsible Party Notification.** The Operator shall notify the Responsible Party immediately, and in any event within twenty-four (24) hours, after becoming aware of any Security Compromise, including any event where there are reasonable grounds to believe that Personal Information has been accessed or acquired by any unauthorised person.

7.2 **Content of Operator Notification.** The Operator's notification shall include:
- (a) a description of the nature of the Security Compromise;
- (b) the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected;
- (c) the categories and approximate number of Personal Information records concerned;
- (d) the name and contact details of the Operator's data protection contact point;
- (e) a description of the likely consequences of the Security Compromise;
- (f) a description of the measures taken or proposed to address the Security Compromise, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

7.3 **Responsible Party Notification Obligations.** The Responsible Party shall, as soon as reasonably possible and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of a Security Compromise:
- (a) notify the Information Regulator through the eServices portal at https://eservices.inforegulator.org.za in the prescribed form; and
- (b) notify affected Data Subjects in writing, providing sufficient information to allow them to take protective measures as required by section 22(5) of POPIA.

7.4 **Cooperation.** The Operator shall:
- (a) cooperate fully with the Responsible Party's investigation of any Security Compromise;
- (b) take immediate steps to contain and remediate the Security Compromise;
- (c) preserve all relevant evidence and logs;
- (d) not communicate with Data Subjects, the Information Regulator, or the media regarding the Security Compromise without the Responsible Party's prior written consent.

7.5 **Records.** The Operator shall maintain a register of all Security Compromises, including those that do not meet the notification threshold, documenting the facts, effects, and remedial actions taken. Such register shall be made available to the Responsible Party upon request.

## 9. CROSS-BORDER TRANSFER OF PERSONAL INFORMATION

9.1 The Operator acknowledges that it processes Personal Information using infrastructure and Sub-Processors located outside the Republic of South Africa, as detailed in Schedule C.

9.2 The Responsible Party authorises such transfers on the following bases under section 72(1) of POPIA:

- (a) **For transfers to jurisdictions with adequate data protection laws** (including EU/EEA member states under the General Data Protection Regulation): The Operator confirms that the recipient jurisdiction provides an adequate level of protection as contemplated in section 72(1)(a), upholds processing principles substantially similar to POPIA, and includes provisions for further transfer restrictions.

- (b) **For transfers to jurisdictions without formal adequacy recognition**: The Operator shall ensure that a binding agreement is in place with each Sub-Processor that provides an adequate level of protection, incorporating at minimum:
  - (i) Standard Contractual Clauses (EU Commission Decision 2021/914);
  - (ii) Processing restrictions equivalent to this Agreement;
  - (iii) Security measures meeting section 19 of POPIA standards;
  - (iv) Immediate breach notification obligations.

9.3 For transfers of Personal Information relating to juristic persons (as protected under POPIA but not under the GDPR), the Operator shall ensure that additional contractual safeguards are in place to provide equivalent protection, including confidentiality obligations, purpose limitation equivalent to POPIA conditions, and Data Subject rights equivalent to Chapter 5 of POPIA.

9.4 The Operator shall not transfer Health Information outside the Republic without the prior written consent of the Responsible Party and in compliance with Regulation 6 of the Health Information Regulations, 2026.

9.5 The Operator shall maintain records of all cross-border transfers, including the identity of recipients, jurisdiction, legal basis relied upon, and applicable safeguards, and shall make such records available to the Responsible Party on request.

9.6 In the event that any transfer mechanism relied upon is invalidated by a court or regulatory authority, the Operator shall promptly implement alternative safeguards or cease the relevant transfers until adequate protections are in place.

## 12. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT

### Availability

12.1 The Operator shall ensure Platform availability of not less than **99.9%** per calendar month, measured as:

> Availability % = ((Total Minutes - Unplanned Downtime Minutes) / Total Minutes) x 100

"Unplanned Downtime" excludes:
- (a) scheduled maintenance (with 48 hours' prior notice);
- (b) force majeure events;
- (c) failures caused by the Responsible Party or its end users;
- (d) third-party service outages beyond the Operator's reasonable control.

### Service Credits

12.2 If monthly availability falls below the committed level:

| Availability | Credit |
|-------------|--------|
| 99.9% to 99.5% | 10% of monthly fee |
| 99.5% to 99.0% | 25% of monthly fee |
| Below 99.0% | 50% of monthly fee |
| Below 98.0% | Right to terminate without penalty |

Service credits are applied against future subscription fees and do not entitle the Responsible Party to a cash refund.

### Support

12.3 The Operator shall provide:

| Priority | Response Time | Resolution Target |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| Critical (system down) | 1 hour | 4 hours |
| High (major feature impaired) | 4 hours | 1 business day |
| Medium (minor feature impaired) | 1 business day | 3 business days |
| Low (general enquiry) | 2 business days | 5 business days |

### Disaster Recovery

12.4 The Operator shall maintain and regularly test a disaster recovery plan providing:
- (a) Recovery Time Objective (RTO): not more than 4 hours for critical systems;
- (b) Recovery Point Objective (RPO): not more than 1 hour (maximum data loss window);
- (c) Automated daily backups with encryption at rest;
- (d) Geographic redundancy (backup in a separate availability zone);
- (e) Annual disaster recovery testing with results available to the Responsible Party on request.

## 13. TERMINATION AND DATA HANDLING

### Termination

13.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement:
- (a) on not less than 30 days' written notice (month-to-month subscription);
- (b) immediately, if the other Party commits a material breach that is not remedied within 30 days of written notice;
- (c) immediately, if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters business rescue, or is placed under liquidation.

13.2 The Responsible Party may terminate this Agreement immediately if:
- (a) the Operator suffers a Security Compromise that the Responsible Party reasonably believes cannot be adequately remediated;
- (b) the Operator engages a Sub-Processor to which the Responsible Party has objected and the objection has not been resolved within 30 days;
- (c) the Operator is subject to an enforcement notice from the Information Regulator that materially affects its ability to perform under this Agreement.

### Data Return and Portability

13.3 Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, the Operator shall, at the Responsible Party's election:
- (a) return all Personal Information to the Responsible Party in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format (including CSV, JSON, or SQL dump) within 30 days of termination; or
- (b) securely destroy all Personal Information and provide written certification of destruction within 30 days of termination.

13.4 The Operator shall provide reasonable assistance with data migration to an alternative service provider, including maintaining read-only access to the Platform for a transition period of not less than 60 days following termination.

### Mandatory Retention Overrides

13.5 Notwithstanding clause 13.3, the Operator acknowledges that certain Personal Information is subject to mandatory retention periods under South African law, including but not limited to:

- (a) **Health Records**: Minimum 6 years from date of last patient contact (HPCSA Guidelines on Patient Records), and:
  - for minors: until the patient's 21st birthday;
  - for mentally incompetent patients: for the patient's lifetime;
  - for occupational health records: minimum 20 years after treatment.

- (b) **Financial Records**: Minimum 5 years (Tax Administration Act 28 of 2011; Companies Act 71 of 2008).

13.6 Where mandatory retention periods extend beyond the termination date, the Operator shall:
- (a) continue to store such Personal Information securely in accordance with this Agreement;
- (b) restrict processing to storage and legally required access only (no active use);
- (c) delete such information promptly upon expiry of the applicable retention period;
- (d) provide annual certification of compliance with retention obligations;
- (e) the Responsible Party shall compensate the Operator for reasonable storage costs during the extended retention period at a rate agreed at the time of termination, not exceeding the lowest subscription tier fee.

13.7 Destruction of Personal Information must be carried out in a manner that prevents its reconstruction in an intelligible form, in compliance with section 14 of POPIA.

### Survival

13.8 Clauses 5 (Health Information Processing), 7 (Security Compromise Notification), 9 (Cross-Border Transfers), 13 (Termination and Data Handling), 14 (Confidentiality), 15 (Liability and Indemnification), and 18 (Regulatory Compliance) shall survive termination or expiry of this Agreement.
