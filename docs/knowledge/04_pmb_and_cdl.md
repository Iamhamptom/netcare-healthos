# 04 — Prescribed Minimum Benefits (PMBs) & Chronic Disease List
## VisioCorp Health Intelligence KB | Compiled 2026-03-21

---

## OVERVIEW

PMBs are benefits that ALL registered medical schemes MUST cover, regardless of plan or benefit option. Three categories:

1. **270 Diagnosis Treatment Pairs (DTPs)** — specific conditions with prescribed treatments
2. **27 Chronic Disease List (CDL)** — chronic conditions requiring ongoing medication
3. **Emergency medical conditions** — any sudden life-threatening condition

---

## PAYMENT RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

| Scenario | Rule |
|----------|------|
| At DSP | Pay in FULL — no co-payment, no deductible |
| At non-DSP (DSP exists) | May impose co-payment, but MUST pay at least DSP rate |
| No DSP appointed | Pay in FULL at ANY provider (CMS v Genesis [2015]) |
| Emergency | ALWAYS in full, ANY provider, no pre-auth |
| During waiting period | PMBs still paid (override waiting period) |
| Benefits exhausted | PMBs CONTINUE from risk pool (override limits) |
| Savings account | PMBs NEVER from PMSA (Regulation 10(6)) |

## 270 DTPs — 15 CATEGORIES

### Category 1: Brain & Nervous System
Stroke (CVA), bacterial meningitis, brain abscess, encephalitis, status epilepticus, extradural/subdural haematoma, hydrocephalus, brain tumour, spinal cord compression, subarachnoid haemorrhage, TB meningitis, acute generalised paralysis (Guillain-Barré)

### Category 2: Cardiovascular
Acute MI (heart attack), aortic aneurysm, cardiac tamponade, acute heart failure, DVT, infective endocarditis, constrictive pericarditis, acute PVD, pulmonary embolism, acute rheumatic fever, valvular heart disease (surgical)

### Category 3: Respiratory
Empyema, lung abscess, bacterial pneumonia, tension pneumothorax, pulmonary TB, acute respiratory failure, upper airway obstruction

### Category 4: Digestive/GI
Acute abdomen (appendicitis, cholecystitis, bowel obstruction, perforation), acute GI bleeding, acute hepatic failure, intestinal obstruction, acute pancreatitis, peritonitis

### Category 5: Musculoskeletal
Fractures (surgical), hip/knee replacement, acute osteomyelitis, septic arthritis, spinal fractures/dislocations

### Category 6: Renal/Urinary
Acute renal failure, obstructive renal calculi, urinary tract obstruction, nephrotic syndrome

### Category 7: Obstetric
Caesarean section, ectopic pregnancy, pre-eclampsia/eclampsia, postpartum haemorrhage, placenta praevia, abruptio placentae, hyperemesis gravidarum, puerperal sepsis

### Category 8: Neonatal
Neonatal sepsis, respiratory distress syndrome, severe jaundice (exchange transfusion), congenital abnormalities (surgical)

### Category 9: ENT
Acute mastoiditis, peritonsillar abscess, tracheostomy

### Category 10: Eye
Acute glaucoma, retinal detachment, penetrating eye injury, orbital cellulitis

### Category 11: Skin
Significant burns, necrotising fasciitis, cellulitis (IV antibiotics)

### Category 12: Endocrine/Metabolic
Diabetic ketoacidosis, Addisonian crisis, thyrotoxic crisis, hypoglycaemic coma, severe malnutrition

### Category 13: Haematological/Infectious
Aplastic anaemia, acute haemolytic anaemia, leukaemia, lymphoma, severe malaria, HIV opportunistic infections, septicaemia, tetanus, rabies PEP

### Category 14: Psychiatric
Major depression (hospitalisation), acute schizophrenia, acute bipolar, attempted suicide/self-harm

### Category 15: Oncology
All cancers requiring definitive treatment (surgery, chemo, radiation) — covered within respective body system categories

**Full DTP list with ICD-10 codes**: medicalschemes.co.za/resources/pmb/pmb-conditions/

## EMERGENCY MEDICAL CONDITIONS

### Legal Definition (Regulation 7)
"Sudden and unexpected onset requiring immediate medical/surgical treatment, where failure would result in serious impairment, dysfunction, or jeopardy to life."

### Rules
- **No DSP restriction** — nearest facility, ANY provider
- **No pre-authorization** — treatment must not be delayed
- **Stabilisation first** — then transfer/authorization discussions
- **No co-payment** — regardless of provider used
- **24-hour rule** — covered in full for first 24 hours until stable

---

## PMB CASE LAW SUMMARY

| Ruling | Key Principle |
|--------|---------------|
| Genesis [2015] | Schemes without DSPs pay PMBs at ANY provider |
| GEMS v Appeal Board [2016] | State-as-DSP still requires proper PMB compliance |
| 11-year review (SAMJ 2019) | 69.4% of PMB appeals won by members. Schemes systematically over-deny. |

## 8-STEP AI FLAGGING ALGORITHM

1. **ICD-10 match**: Screen against 2022 CMS PMB Coded List
2. **DTP validation**: Verify condition meets Annexure A descriptor
3. **CDL check**: Flag any of 27 CDL conditions for chronic benefit
4. **Emergency detection**: Flag trauma/acute ICD-10 codes
5. **Underpayment detection**: Shortfall on confirmed PMB = flagged
6. **Savings account alert**: PMB debited from PMSA = illegal
7. **Co-payment validation**: Co-pay on PMB at DSP = illegal
8. **Benefit limit monitoring**: PMBs must continue above limits

## COMMON SCHEME TACTICS TO AVOID PMB PAYMENT

1. Deflecting PMB costs to savings accounts (illegal)
2. Applying monetary caps to PMB conditions (illegal post-depletion)
3. Narrow DSP networks making access difficult
4. Restrictive formularies excluding effective medications
5. Misclassifying PMB conditions through coding manipulation
6. Delaying authorization for emergencies
7. Imposing co-payments where Regulation 8 prohibits them
8. Requiring excessive documentation to delay/discourage
9. Not informing members of PMB entitlements

---

## PMB COMPLAINTS DATA
- **~2,000 PMB complaints/year** to CMS (~40% of ALL complaints)
- **R40 billion** paid out-of-pocket by members for denied claims annually
- **>50% of complainants win** at the regulator
- Most disputed: fractures, diabetes, cancers, emergencies, prosthetics
