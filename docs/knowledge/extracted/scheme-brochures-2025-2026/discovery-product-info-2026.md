contribution increase strategy for 2026
Discovery Health Medical Scheme contribution increases for 2026
In 2026, Discovery Health Medical Scheme has set a weighted average contribution increase of 7.2%, with 65% of members experiencing the lower 6.9% increase, and a 
0.0% increase for members on the Active Smart plan. Contributions for the balance of members will increase by 7.9%, reflecting the higher impact of supply- and demand-
side utilisation on their plans. The Scheme’s strong projected solvency position of 31.6% at the end of 2025 allows the contribution increases to be deferred until 1 April 
2026, providing members with financial relief during the first three months of 2026. By maintaining 2025 contribution levels for the first three months, members will 
collectively save R1.5 billion – around R1,100 per average policy.
RETURNING EXCESS RESERVES TO MEMBERS THROUGH RESPONSIBLE SOLVENCY MANAGEMENT
Discovery Health Medical Scheme has consistently demonstrated market-leading financial discipline, managing solvency through effective pricing strategies that align contributions 
with medical inflation while responsibly returning some excess reserves to members. This approach proved especially important during the unprecedented challenges of the 2020-
2023 COVID years, ensuring stability and protection for members when it mattered most.
The Scheme is projected to end 2025 with a solvency level of 31.6%, well above regulatory requirements. This strength has enabled the Scheme to defer the 2026 contribution 
increase to 1 April, directly benefiting members with R1.5 billion in collective savings. This responsible approach reflects the Scheme’s leadership in ensuring short-term affordability 
relief for members, while maintaining the long-term sustainability and strength of the Scheme.

Care programmes for KeyCare
Members living with a chronic illness are at greater risk of episodes of poor health, including hospital 
admissions for the treatment of complications related to their illness. Discovery Health Medical 
Scheme members with chronic illnesses who use a single GP for the coordination of their long term 
care have a 24% lower hospital admission rate compared with members who use multiple GPs. 
Based on the significant opportunity to improve health outcomes through care coordination, the 
following changes will be made to the KeyCare series for 2026: 
	
Members who are registered on the relevant Discovery Health Medical Scheme Care Programme 
will continue to receive full cover up to 100% of the Discovery Health Rate for all approved basket 
of care healthcare services for their chronic condition. 
	
Members who are not registered on a relevant Care Programme will receive cover up to 80% of 
the Discovery Health Rate from the Chronic Illness Benefit baskets of care, with the co-payment 
payable by the member. 
This applies to members registered for major depression as a Prescribed Minimum Benefit and 
members registered on the Chronic Illness Benefit for diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, 
ischaemic heart disease and HIV.

NURTURE AT HOME 
Nurture at Home is a new benefit programme designed to support parents whose new-born baby had 
to spend an extended period of time in a Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) due to premature birth 
or medical complications before going home. 
While NICUs provide intensive, life-saving care for vulnerable newborns, the moment of discharge 
marks the beginning of a new phase for families. Parents need to manage their baby’s complex 
medical, developmental, and emotional needs, often without adequate support or guidance. 
This programme will include a supporting basket of benefits to families whose newborn spent  
7 or more days in NICU before discharge, including, but not limited to: 
	
A pre-discharge overnight stay in hospital for one of the parents registered on the Scheme 
	
Virtual health coaching sessions 
	
A virtual and home nurse visit 
	
Mental health support 
	
Follow-up paediatrician visits. 
Available on all Discovery Health Medical Scheme plans except Essential Smart, Essential Dynamic 
Smart, Active Smart and KeyCare plans. Nuture at Home is subject to the Scheme’s clinical entry 
criteria, treatment guidelines and managed care protocols. 
PERINATAL BEREAVEMENT COUNSELLING 
The emotional needs following pregnancy loss are distinct from other types of loss and early, tailored, 
and trauma-informed support is especially effective in guiding families through their loss. Discovery 
Health offers members of Discovery Health Medical Scheme access to perinatal bereavement 
counselling aimed at supporting expecting families who have suffered the sad loss of pregnancy, 
experienced a still birth or loss of their baby immediately post birth. Members will be able to access 
this support through the Women’s Health Hub integration.

OPTIMISING THE ONCOLOGY BENEFIT 
Oncology Innovation Benefit 
The Oncology Innovation Benefit offers cover for a defined list of non-PMB novel and ultra high-cost cancer 
medicines such as immunotherapies, monoclonal antibodies and targeted therapies for specified conditions. 
Since it was introduced in 2019, the Oncology Innovation Benefit has covered nearly R1 billion in treatment 
costs, expanding cover from 7 molecules and 2 conditions to 11 molecules and 20 conditions in 2025.
During this time, the annual growth rate in claimants and costs has been more than 45%, despite  
a reduction in the number of members on the plans offering the Oncology Innovation Benefit.
To maintain access to the treatment options enabled by the Oncology Innovation Benefit, the 
following changes to the benefit will be implemented in 2026:
01.	 The co-payment on treatment will apply to all molecules and conditions.
02.	 The co-payment on treatment will be 50% for members of the Comprehensive Series.
03.	 The co-payment on treatment will be 30% for members of the Executive Plan. 
These co-payments will only be applicable to all new treatment plan approvals from 2026 onwards. 
Members on existing treatment plans will therefore remain at the same level of co-payment as in 2025.
