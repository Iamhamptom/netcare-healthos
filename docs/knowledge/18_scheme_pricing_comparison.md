# 18 — Scheme Pricing Comparison: What SA Medical Schemes Pay for the Same Procedures

> Compiled: 2026-03-21
> Purpose: Sales intelligence for practices — which schemes pay better, faster, and with fewer rejections
> Sources: CMS Industry Report 2024, ASAIPA Tariff Comparison 2025, scheme benefit brochures 2025/2026, ECIPA/IPAF tariff guides, Medprax, medicalaid.com, scheme provider portals
> **Note**: Exact rand amounts change annually (typically 5-9% increases). This document provides relative positioning and ranges. Always confirm current rates via your practice management software or scheme provider portal.

## SECTION A: CONSULTATION RATE COMPARISON

### How Scheme Rates Work

SA medical schemes no longer use a single "NHRPL" (National Health Reference Price List). Since the Competition Commission's 2014 ruling, each scheme sets its own rates independently. Key terminology:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **DHR** | Discovery Health Rate — Discovery's proprietary tariff schedule |
| **Scheme Rate** | The maximum a scheme will reimburse per tariff code |
| **100% of Scheme Rate** | Full reimbursement at the scheme's published rate |
| **200%/300% of Scheme Rate** | Multiple of scheme rate (typically for specialists) |
| **Network Rate** | Negotiated (usually lower) rate for DSP/network providers |
| **GEMS Tariff** | GEMS publishes its own tariff files annually (publicly available) |

### 1. GP Consultation — Standard (Code 0190)

**Industry Average (CMS 2024):** R515 per event (out-of-hospital GP), up 5.18% from R490 in 2023.

| Scheme | Estimated 2025/26 Rate Range | Network Differential | Notes |
|--------|------------------------------|---------------------|-------|
| **Discovery** | R520–R580 (DHR) | Network GP: 100% DHR; Non-network: 80% DHR | KeyCare plans: MUST use KeyCare GP — non-network = 0% cover. Classic/Comprehensive plans more generous. Co-pay R75–R130 on some plans |
| **GEMS** | R480–R540 | State hospital = DSP; contracted GPs paid at GEMS tariff | Published tariff files on gems.gov.za. Stricter consultation limits. Sapphire/Ruby have day-to-day sub-limits |
| **Bonitas** | R500–R560 | Network GP preferred; non-network covered from savings | BonComprehensive top tier. BonCap/BonStart have strict network requirements. Transitioning to Momentum Health Solutions admin from June 2026 |
| **Momentum** | R490–R550 | Incentive/Custom plans: network GP required | Pre-2026 administered by Momentum Health Solutions. Ingwe plan: strict network |
| **Medihelp** | R510–R570 | MedPlus/MedElect: more generous; MedSaver: network required | Self-administered since 1906. Generally competitive rates |
| **Bestmed** | R500–R560 | Beat plan: network only; Pulse/Pace: more flexible | Self-administered. Co-payment R400 follow-up / R1,300 first consultation reported at some practices |

**Practice Insight:** Discovery and Medihelp tend to offer the highest GP rates, but Discovery's network requirements (especially on KeyCare/Smart plans) can restrict access. GEMS is typically 5-10% below Discovery's DHR.

### 2. Specialist Consultation — Initial (Code 0191/0192)

**Industry Average (CMS 2024):** R1,700 per event (out-of-hospital medical specialist), up 5.75% from R1,607.

| Scheme | Rate Positioning | Specialist Payment Model |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Discovery** | Highest — up to 300% DHR for non-contracted specialists on Comprehensive/Executive plans | Payment arrangement specialists: agreed rate. No arrangement: 100-300% DHR depending on plan tier |
| **GEMS** | Below industry average | Contracted specialists at GEMS tariff. Non-contracted: reduced rate. Emerald Value: more restrictions |
| **Bonitas** | Mid-range | BonComprehensive: generous. GP referral required on Standard/Standard Select plans |
| **Momentum** | Mid-range | Summit: 200% scheme rate. Custom/Incentive: network preferred |
| **Medihelp** | Competitive | MedPlus: up to 200% scheme rate. Higher tiers more generous |
| **Bestmed** | Below mid-range | Pace: up to 200%. Beat: network only |

**Practice Insight:** Discovery Executive and Comprehensive plans pay the highest specialist rates in the industry (up to 300% DHR). For practices targeting specialist referrals, Discovery members on top-tier plans are the most lucrative patients.

### 3. After-Hours Consultation

After-hours modifiers (modifier 0002 for after-hours, 0003 for weekends/public holidays) increase the base consultation rate.

| Scheme | After-Hours Policy | Modifier Value |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Discovery** | Covered at DHR + modifier. ER visits covered differently from rooms consultations | Typically 50-100% uplift on base rate |
| **GEMS** | Covered but strict — pre-auth may be required for hospital ER | GEMS tariff + modifier. More restrictive on Onyx/Ruby |
| **Bonitas** | Covered from relevant benefit (savings or risk) | Standard modifier applies |
| **Momentum** | Covered; network ER preferred | Emergency: covered from hospital benefit. Rooms: from day-to-day |
| **Medihelp** | Generally covered with modifier | Competitive modifier rates |
| **Bestmed** | Covered; ER co-payments may apply | Standard modifier |

**Practice Insight:** After-hours claims are frequently queried across all schemes. Always document clinical justification and time of consultation. Discovery and GEMS are the strictest on after-hours auditing.

### 4. Telehealth / Virtual Consultation

| Scheme | Telehealth Policy | Rate vs In-Person |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Discovery** | Actively promoted — DrConnect, Smart Network GP video consultations. Covered in full up to DHR on Smart plans | Equal to or slightly below in-person rate. Code 0190V or modifier |
| **GEMS** | Accepted post-COVID; specific codes required | Below in-person rate (typically 70-80% of face-to-face) |
| **Bonitas** | BonCore (new 2026) is digital-first — telehealth is primary modality | BonCore: full cover for virtual. Other plans: accepted but limited |
| **Momentum** | HelloDoctor partnership. Summit/Custom: covered | Covered at scheme rate |
| **Medihelp** | Accepted; growing acceptance | Covered at reduced rate |
| **Bestmed** | Accepted on higher-tier plans | Limited coverage on Beat plan |

**Practice Insight:** Discovery is the most telehealth-friendly scheme. Bonitas BonCore (R1,275/beneficiary, ages 22-35) is entirely digital-first — a new model worth watching.

### 5. Follow-Up Consultation (Code 0192)

| Scheme | Follow-Up Rate | Relative to Initial |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Discovery** | R380–R450 (estimated) | ~70-75% of initial consultation DHR |
| **GEMS** | R350–R420 | ~70-75% of GEMS tariff |
| **Bonitas** | R370–R440 | ~70-75% of initial |
| **Momentum** | R360–R430 | ~70-75% of initial |
| **Medihelp** | R380–R450 | ~70-75% of initial |
| **Bestmed** | R360–R430 | ~70-75% of initial |

**Practice Insight:** Follow-up rates are consistently 25-30% lower than initial consultation rates across all schemes. Some schemes limit the number of follow-up visits per condition per year.

## SECTION B: PROCEDURE RATE COMPARISON

### 6. Common In-Rooms Procedures

| Procedure | Industry Avg Paid (2024) | Discovery | GEMS | Bonitas | Notes |
|-----------|------------------------|-----------|------|---------|-------|
| **ECG (code 1220)** | R350–R450 | DHR: competitive | GEMS tariff: slightly below | Covered from day-to-day or risk | Pre-auth not usually required |
| **Spirometry (code 1236)** | R400–R550 | DHR: mid-high | GEMS tariff | Covered | May need ICD-10 justification on repeat claims |
| **Wound suturing (code 0310-0314)** | R600–R1,200 | DHR + materials | GEMS tariff + materials | Covered from risk/savings | Complexity-dependent. Discovery pays well for procedural codes. Materials billed separately |

**Practice Insight:** Discovery generally pays the highest procedural rates. Practices that do more in-rooms procedures (ECG, spirometry, minor surgery) earn significantly more per Discovery patient than per GEMS patient.

| Test | NAPPI/Code | Discovery | GEMS | Bonitas | Private Lab Charge |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|---------|-------------------|

| **U&E (Urea & Electrolytes)** | 3758+ | R180–R240 | R170–R220 | R175–R230 | R250–R350 |

### 8. Radiology Rates

**Industry Average (CMS 2024):** R1,580 per event (out-of-hospital radiology), R2,830 per event (in-hospital).

| Procedure | Discovery (est.) | GEMS (est.) | Bonitas (est.) | Typical Private Charge |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Chest X-ray (2 views)** | R550–R700 | R500–R650 | R520–R670 | R800–R1,200 |
| **Ultrasound (abdominal)** | R1,200–R1,800 | R1,100–R1,600 | R1,150–R1,700 | R1,800–R2,800 |
| **CT scan (body)** | R3,500–R6,000 | R3,200–R5,500 | R3,300–R5,800 | R5,000–R10,000 |
| **MRI (body)** | R6,000–R12,000 | R5,500–R10,000 | R5,800–R11,000 | R8,000–R18,000 |

**Key scheme-specific rules:**
- **Discovery**: MRI/CT requires pre-authorisation. Co-payment R3,850 for out-of-hospital scans on some plans; reduced if using value-based provider. KeyCare: R5,550 limit for radiology per person per year
- **GEMS**: Pre-auth mandatory for CT/MRI/PET. State facility preferred (DSP). Gastrointestinal scopes: new co-payment introduced 2026
- **Bonitas**: Pre-auth for CT/MRI. BonComprehensive most generous. Hospital plans: limited to PMB only
- **Momentum**: Pre-auth for MRI/CT/PET/MRCP. Network radiology preferred
- **Medihelp**: Pre-auth for advanced imaging
- **Bestmed**: Pre-auth required; annual limits apply

**Practice Insight:** Radiology is one of the highest-cost service categories. Discovery pays the best rates but imposes the strictest pre-auth and value-based provider incentives. GEMS consistently pays 10-15% below Discovery for the same procedures.

### 9. Dental Rates

**Industry Average (CMS 2024):** R1,393 per dental event (out-of-hospital), R3,456 per event (in-hospital). Dental specialist events: R2,163 (out-of-hospital), R6,492 (in-hospital).

| Procedure | Discovery (est.) | GEMS (est.) | Bonitas (est.) | Momentum Summit |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Scale & polish** | R550–R700 | R500–R650 | R520–R680 | R530–R690 |
| **Filling (1 surface)** | R450–R600 | R400–R550 | R420–R580 | R430–R590 |
| **Extraction (simple)** | R400–R550 | R370–R520 | R380–R530 | R390–R540 |
| **Root canal (anterior)** | R2,500–R4,000 | R2,200–R3,500 | R2,300–R3,700 | R2,400–R3,800 |

**Dental benefit structures:**
- **Discovery**: Annual dental check-up covered (consultation + bitewing X-rays + scale & polish + fluoride). Co-payment R125 (Classic Smart) or R190 (Essential Smart) per check-up. Balance at DHR. Advanced dentistry from specialist benefit
- **GEMS**: Dental covered from day-to-day benefit. Emerald: R5,760 dental limit. Annual limits vary by option
- **Bonitas**: Covered once every 2 years from risk on some plans. BonComprehensive: annual dental benefit
- **Momentum Summit**: Basic dentistry from R34,500 day-to-day limit. Advanced dentistry: R8,530/beneficiary or R25,470/family annual limit
- **Medihelp**: Dental from day-to-day or savings. Annual limits apply
- **Bestmed**: Dental included in day-to-day allocation. Annual limits per plan

## SECTION D: PAYMENT BEHAVIOR

### 16. Average Days-to-Payment

| Scheme | Typical Payment Turnaround | Payment Runs | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------|-------------|-------|
| **Discovery** | 14–21 days (electronic) | Weekly EFT runs | Fastest electronic payer in the industry. Auto-adjudication rate: 78%. Complex claims: 30+ days |
| **GEMS** | 21–30 days | 2x monthly (mid-month + end-month) | 60-day dispute turnaround (longest in industry). Slower than Discovery |
| **Bonitas** | 14–21 days | Bi-weekly | Currently Medscheme-administered (efficient). Transition to Momentum from June 2026 may temporarily affect turnaround |
| **Momentum** | 14–28 days | Bi-weekly | Generally reliable. Network claims faster |
| **Medihelp** | 21–30 days | Monthly | Self-administered — slightly slower. Submission deadline: last workday of 4th month |
| **Bestmed** | 21–30 days | Bi-weekly | Self-administered. Generally reliable |

**Practice Insight:** Discovery pays fastest due to its 78% auto-adjudication rate. GEMS is consistently the slowest, with the longest dispute turnaround (60 days). Bonitas's transition to Momentum administration in June 2026 is a risk — practices should monitor payment timelines closely during the transition period.

### 17. Average Rejection Rate

| Scheme | Estimated Rejection Rate | Most Common Rejection Reasons | Risk Level for Practice |
|--------|------------------------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Discovery** | 8–12% | Code 59 (tariff incorrect), Code 02 (account required), Code 04 (auth letter required), Code 54 (illegible) | **Medium-High** — strict ICD-10 specificity requirements. Maximum 4th character mandatory |
| **GEMS** | 12–18% | Tariff code mismatch, benefit exhausted, no pre-auth, consultation limit exceeded | **High** — stricter consultation limits, rigid PMB interpretation. Missed appointments NOT covered |
| **Bonitas** | 8–12% | Off-formulary medication, GP referral missing (Standard/Standard Select), benefit exhausted | **Medium** |
| **Momentum** | 10–14% | No pre-auth, PMB letter not submitted, network violation | **Medium** — requires formal PMB letter from practice |
| **Medihelp** | 8–12% | Submission deadline missed, coding errors | **Medium** |
| **Bestmed** | 8–12% | Benefit limit exceeded, network violation (Beat plan) | **Medium-Low** |

**Practice Insight:** GEMS has the highest rejection rate in the industry. Practices with a high proportion of GEMS patients need rigorous pre-submission validation. Discovery's ICD-10 specificity requirements catch many practices — always code to the 4th character.

### 19. Clawback Frequency

| Scheme | Clawback Risk | Known Incidents | Practice Impact |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Discovery** | **HIGH — Industry worst** | January 2026: R130–R170M clawback from pharmacy overpayments (eventually reversed after CMS intervention and public outcry). Known to claw back months/years after initial payment. MediCheck accused Discovery of "breaking the law" with delayed clawbacks | Practices must maintain reserves for potential clawbacks. Discovery's Rules allow 30-day investigation period for disputes |
| **GEMS** | **Low-Medium** | Less frequent than Discovery | Government scheme — more predictable |
| **Bonitas** | **Low** | Rare | Medscheme administration generally stable |
| **Momentum** | **Low** | Rare | Generally predictable |
| **Medihelp** | **Very Low** | Very rare | Self-administered, conservative approach |
| **Bestmed** | **Very Low** | Very rare | Self-administered, conservative approach |

**Practice Insight:** Discovery's clawback risk is the single biggest financial risk for practices with high Discovery patient loads. The January 2026 incident (R130–R170M attempted recovery) demonstrated that Discovery can and will reverse payments long after the fact. Practices should: (1) maintain a clawback reserve of 2-5% of Discovery revenue, (2) dispute aggressively within 30 days, (3) involve the CMS if clawbacks are unreasonable.

### 20. PMB Compliance — Who Underpays PMBs Most?

| Scheme | PMB Compliance | Known Issues |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Discovery** | **Good overall, but aggressive cost management** | Uses formularies and treatment baskets to manage PMB costs. Will fund PMBs but steers to DSPs and formulary options. Members must request PMB funding explicitly |
| **GEMS** | **Below average** | Rigid PMB interpretation. State hospital as DSP for PMBs means members must go to state facilities for full PMB cover. Non-DSP = co-payment or reduced rate. CMS has received complaints |
| **Bonitas** | **Average** | Managed care protocols for PMBs. Will fund but requires pre-auth and adherence to protocols. Regulation 15H/15I: will cover substitution treatment if formulary drug is ineffective |
| **Momentum** | **Below average** | Practice must send letter requesting PMB on patient's behalf. Administrative burden placed on provider |
| **Medihelp** | **Average** | Generally compliant but can be slow to approve |
| **Bestmed** | **Average** | Generally compliant |

**Practice Insight:** GEMS and Momentum are the biggest PMB challenges. GEMS's insistence on state facilities as DSP for PMBs is a major barrier. Momentum's requirement for a formal PMB letter from the practice creates administrative overhead. Discovery is better but uses formulary restrictions to control costs.

## SECTION G: PRACTICE REVENUE OPTIMIZATION BY SCHEME

### Revenue Per Patient — Scheme Ranking (Highest to Lowest)

1. **Discovery Executive/Comprehensive** — Highest rates, most generous benefits, fastest payment. But: clawback risk, strict ICD-10 coding
2. **Bonitas BonComprehensive** — Competitive rates, 61 CDL conditions, good payment turnaround. Risk: admin transition June 2026
3. **Medihelp MedElect Plus/MedPlus** — Competitive rates, low clawback risk, reliable payment
4. **Momentum Summit** — Good rates, adequate benefits. Moderate payment speed
5. **Bestmed Pulse** — Mid-range rates, reliable payment, surplus scheme (financially healthy)
6. **Discovery Smart/KeyCare** — Low rates, strict network, co-payments. Network GPs only
7. **GEMS Emerald/Beryl** — Below-average rates, slowest payment, highest rejection rate. But: 2.4M lives = volume play
8. **GEMS Onyx/Ruby** — Lowest rates, most restrictive benefits

### Key Takeaways for Sales Conversations

1. **"Which scheme pays best?"** — Discovery (top plans) pays 10-20% above industry average. GEMS pays 10-15% below. But volume matters: GEMS has 2.4M lives vs Discovery's 2.7M

2. **"Which scheme pays fastest?"** — Discovery (14-21 days), then Bonitas/Momentum (14-28 days). GEMS is slowest (21-30 days, 60-day dispute resolution)

3. **"Which scheme rejects most?"** — GEMS (12-18% rejection rate). Discovery is strict but has lower rejection rates due to auto-adjudication

4. **"Which scheme is the biggest risk?"** — Discovery for clawbacks, GEMS for rejections and slow payment, Bonitas for admin transition uncertainty (2026)

5. **"Should I join a scheme's network?"** — If >30% of your patients are from one scheme, joining the network increases volume but locks in lower rates. Calculate the trade-off: 15% more patients at 10% lower rates = net positive only if your capacity is underutilised
