### 1.2 Three-Step Supplier Adoption Process

Netcare follows a structured three-step process:

#### Step 1: Accreditation (Questionnaire)

All prospective suppliers must complete the **Netcare Supplier Accreditation Questionnaire** electronically via the Netcare Suppliers portal.

- Netcare evaluates the questionnaire
- **Accreditation pass mark**: 75% and above
- Netcare will contact successful applicants when goods/services are needed, or retain details for future opportunities
- If you score below 75%, you can re-apply after addressing deficiencies

**Portal**: [https://www.netcare.co.za/Netcare-Suppliers/Suppliers-Accreditation-Form](https://www.netcare.co.za/Netcare-Suppliers/Suppliers-Accreditation-Form)

#### Step 2: Supplier Application (Full Documentation)

Approved candidates complete the **online Supplier Application Form** with required documentation. Key requirements:

| Document | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Company registration** | CIPC registration certificate (CK or CoR documents) |
| **VAT registration** | VAT registration certificate (if turnover exceeds R1M) |
| **Tax clearance** | Valid SARS Tax Compliance Status (TCS) PIN or certificate |
| **B-BBEE certificate** | Current B-BBEE certificate or EME/QSE sworn affidavit |
| **Bank confirmation** | Bank-stamped letter confirming account details (not older than 3 months) |
| **Directors/members** | ID copies of all directors/members |
| **No judgments declaration** | Declaration that no judgments exist against the company |
| **Industry certifications** | Any registration certificates pertaining to your relevant industry |
| **Trade association membership** | Membership certificates for relevant trade associations |
| **Insurance certificates** | Professional indemnity, cyber liability (as applicable) |
| **Reference letters** | At least 2-3 client reference letters |

**CRITICAL**: Submissions are restricted to **50 pages**. Outdated information leads to disqualification or payment issues.

**Policy reference**: Netcare Procurement Supplier Accreditation Policy PRC02 and Standard Operating Procedure PR-SOP-005

**Sources**: [Netcare Suppliers page](https://www.netcare.co.za/Netcare-Suppliers) | [PRC02 Policy PDF](https://www.netcare.co.za/Portals/0/Investor%20Relations/Governance/JSE%20SRI/Environmental%20Survey/E14_Supplier_Accredition_Policy.pdf) | [PR-SOP-005 PDF](https://www.netcare.co.za/Portals/0/Investor%20Relations/Policies/PR-SOP-005-Supplier-Accreditation.pdf) | [Supplier Management Policy](https://www.netcare.co.za/Portals/0/Images/Content-Images/suppliers/policies/supplier-management-policy.pdf)

#### Step 3: Terms and Code of Conduct

Once accredited, suppliers must sign:

1. **Netcare Terms and Conditions** (January 2024 version) — requires authorized representative signature with witness signatures on each page
2. **Supplier Code of Conduct** — transparency, integrity, environmental sustainability commitments

### 1.3 B-BBEE Requirements for Netcare Suppliers

Netcare itself is a **Level 2 B-BBEE contributor** with a **110% procurement recognition level** (verified by Empowerdex, certificate issued December 2024, expiring December 2025).

As a JSE-listed entity, Netcare is measured on its **Preferential Procurement** scorecard element. This means:
- Netcare earns B-BBEE points by procuring from higher-level B-BBEE suppliers
- **Level 1 suppliers** give Netcare **135% procurement recognition** (most valuable)
- **Level 2 suppliers** give **125% recognition**
- **Level 4** gives **100% recognition**
- Below Level 4, recognition drops rapidly

**Bottom line**: The higher your B-BBEE level, the more attractive you are to Netcare's procurement team. **Level 1-2 is strongly preferred**; Level 4 is the practical minimum for enterprise contracts.

**Source**: [Netcare B-BBEE Certificate](https://www.netcare.co.za/Netcare-Investor-Relations/Governance/Netcare-B-BBEE-Certificate)

## 2. Legal Documents Needed

### 2.1 Master Services Agreement (MSA)

The MSA is the **foundational contract** between the SaaS vendor and Netcare. For a JSE-listed hospital group, the MSA must cover:

| Section | Must Include |
|---------|-------------|
| **Scope of services** | Detailed description of the platform, modules, deployment model (cloud/hybrid) |
| **Term and renewal** | Initial term (typically 3-5 years for enterprise), auto-renewal provisions, termination notice periods |
| **Fees and payment** | Pricing model, payment schedule, annual escalation (CPI-linked or fixed %), currency |
| **Intellectual property** | Ownership of platform IP (vendor retains), ownership of customizations, data ownership (client retains) |
| **Warranties** | Platform performance, virus-free, non-infringement of third-party IP |
| **Limitation of liability** | Typical cap at 12 months' fees; carve-outs for IP infringement, data breach, death/personal injury |
| **Indemnification** | Mutual indemnification; vendor indemnifies for IP claims, client indemnifies for misuse |
| **Confidentiality** | Incorporated by NDA reference or standalone provisions |
| **Dispute resolution** | Arbitration (AFSA rules) preferred over litigation for speed; governing law = SA law |
| **Force majeure** | Standard clause covering acts of God, government action, pandemic |
| **Insurance requirements** | Minimum cover amounts vendor must maintain |
| **Compliance** | POPIA, HPCSA, King IV governance, B-BBEE commitments |
| **Change management** | Process for scope changes, feature requests, version upgrades |
| **Exit and transition** | Data portability, transition assistance period (typically 90-180 days), data destruction certification |

**Recommendation**: Engage a SA technology law firm (e.g., Michalsons, Webber Wentzel, ENSafrica, or Adams & Adams) to draft or review. Budget R50,000-R150,000 for a comprehensive MSA.

### 2.2 Data Processing Agreement (DPA) / Operator Agreement

**Required under POPIA** when processing personal information (especially health data) on behalf of Netcare.

| Clause | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Parties** | Responsible Party (Netcare) and Operator (Visio Health OS) |
| **Purpose limitation** | Data processed only per Netcare's documented instructions |
| **Categories of data** | Patient demographics, health records, billing data, medical scheme info |
| **Security measures** | Encryption at rest and in transit, access controls, audit logging, penetration testing |
| **Sub-processors** | List all sub-processors (Supabase, Vercel, Anthropic); require prior written consent for changes |
| **Breach notification** | Operator must notify Responsible Party **immediately** upon discovering a compromise; Responsible Party then notifies Information Regulator and data subjects within **72 hours** (per Regulator guidance, though POPIA says "as soon as reasonably possible") |
| **Data retention** | Retention periods per healthcare record-keeping requirements (minimum 5-6 years for medical records) |
| **Data return/deletion** | Upon termination, return all data in agreed format and certify destruction |
| **Audit rights** | Netcare may audit Operator's data processing facilities and practices |
| **Cross-border transfers** | Declare all international data transfers (e.g., to Vercel US regions); ensure adequate protection per POPIA Section 72 |
| **Liability** | Operator liable for damage caused by non-compliance with DPA terms |

**Template**: The Information Regulator has not published an official DPA template, but SA-compliant templates are available from Michalsons, Genie AI, and xneelo.

**Sources**: [POPIA Operator requirements](https://popia.co.za/) | [Genie AI DPA template](https://www.genieai.co/en-za/template/dpa-agreement) | [Michalsons DPA guidance](https://www.michalsons.com/focus-areas/privacy-and-data-protection/information-officer-popi-paia)

### 2.3 Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Healthcare IT SLAs must be rigorous due to patient safety implications:

| Metric | Target | Penalty |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Platform availability** | 99.9% uptime (excludes scheduled maintenance) | Service credits: 5% per 0.1% below target |
| **Scheduled maintenance** | Maximum 4 hours/month, during off-peak (02:00-06:00 SAST) | 48-hour advance notification |
| **Unscheduled downtime** | Maximum 1 hour per incident | Escalation to VP Engineering within 15 minutes |
| **Critical incident response** | P1 (system down): 15 minutes acknowledgment, 1 hour resolution target | Penalty escalation per hour |
| **Major incident response** | P2 (major feature down): 30 minutes acknowledgment, 4 hours resolution | Service credits |
| **Minor incident response** | P3 (minor issue): 2 hours acknowledgment, 24 hours resolution | Tracked in KPIs |
| **Data backup** | Daily automated backups, 30-day retention, quarterly restore testing | Audit requirement |
| **Disaster recovery** | RPO: 1 hour, RTO: 4 hours | Documented DR plan required |
| **Support hours** | Business hours (08:00-17:00 SAST) standard; 24/7 for P1 | Staffing commitment |
| **Reporting** | Monthly uptime report, quarterly service review, annual penetration test | Contractual deliverable |

**Source**: [Healthcare SLA best practices](https://www.whatsupgold.com/blog/meet-slas-health-care) | [SA Treasury SLA guidelines](https://www.treasury.gov.za/espf/LinkedDocuments/S6(6.1)%20SPF%20GPG%20-%20Draft%20Service%20Level%20Agreements.pdf)

### 2.4 Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

Required before any access to Netcare's systems, data, or internal processes.

**Key provisions**:
- **Mutual NDA** preferred (both parties share confidential information)
- **Definition of confidential information**: Include patient data, system architecture, pricing, business strategies
- **Duration**: Obligations survive 3-5 years after termination (indefinite for patient data)
- **Permitted disclosure**: Only to employees/contractors who need to know, bound by similar obligations
- **Return of information**: All confidential materials returned or destroyed upon request/termination
- **Remedies**: Injunctive relief available (monetary damages insufficient for data breaches)
- **Governing law**: South African law; jurisdiction of Gauteng High Court

**Source**: [SA NDA templates](https://ideanav.co.za/free-template-confidentiality-agreements/) | [TIA NDA template](https://www.tia.org.za/storage/2024/05/2024-NDA-template_AW-review_2024-04-25-Clean-Version.pdf)

### 2.5 Business Associate Agreement (BAA) for Health Data

While "BAA" is a US (HIPAA) concept, the SA equivalent is the **Operator Agreement under POPIA** (covered in section 2.2 above). However, if Netcare has international partners or medical scheme relationships that require HIPAA-adjacent compliance, a BAA-style agreement may be requested covering:

- Use and disclosure limitations for Protected Health Information (PHI)
- Safeguards commitment (administrative, physical, technical)
- Breach reporting obligations
- Subcontractor chain compliance
- Individual rights support (access, amendment, accounting of disclosures)

**Recommendation**: Build BAA-equivalent provisions into the DPA rather than a separate document, unless specifically requested by Netcare's legal team.

## 3. B-BBEE Certification

### 3.1 Enterprise Classifications

| Category | Annual Turnover | Verification |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| **EME** (Exempted Micro Enterprise) | R0 — R10 million | Sworn affidavit (free via CIPC) |
| **QSE** (Qualifying Small Enterprise) | R10 million — R50 million | SANAS-accredited verification agency |
| **Large Enterprise** | R50 million+ | Full SANAS verification required |

**Touchline Agency (Pty) Ltd** almost certainly qualifies as an **EME** (turnover under R10M), which means:
- **No costly verification process** — only a sworn affidavit or CIPC certificate needed
- **Free B-BBEE certificate** from CIPC
- Automatic classification based on black ownership percentage

### 3.2 Automatic Levels for Black-Owned EMEs

| Black Ownership | Automatic B-BBEE Level | Procurement Recognition |
|----------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **100% black owned** | **Level 1** | **135%** |
| **51%+ black owned** | **Level 2** | **125%** |
| **Less than 51% black owned** | **Level 4** | **100%** |
| **Non-compliant** | **Level 8 or unrated** | **10% or 0%** |

**For Dr. Hampton (100% black owned)**: Touchline Agency automatically qualifies as **Level 1 B-BBEE**, giving Netcare **135% procurement recognition** — the maximum possible. This is a **massive competitive advantage** in enterprise procurement.

### 3.3 How to Get Certified

**For EMEs (under R10M turnover) — the simple path:**

1. **Download the sworn affidavit template** from CIPC ([https://www.cipc.co.za/?page_id=3612](https://www.cipc.co.za/?page_id=3612)) or the dtic website
2. **Complete the affidavit** with company details, turnover, ownership structure
3. **Have it commissioned** by a Commissioner of Oaths (at any police station or attorney — free at SAPS)
4. **Upload to CIPC** for the free B-BBEE certificate, OR
5. **Apply online** via CIPC eServices portal ([https://eservices.cipc.co.za/](https://eservices.cipc.co.za/))

**Cost**: Free (CIPC route) or R500-R2,000 (if using a service provider for convenience)
**Timeline**: 1-5 business days via CIPC online; same-day if using sworn affidavit directly
**Validity**: 12 months from date of issue — must renew annually

**For QSEs (R10M-R50M — future state):**
- Must use a **SANAS-accredited verification agency**
- Cost: R8,000-R25,000 depending on agency and complexity
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks standard; 2-3 weeks urgent
- Must comply with at least 2 priority elements: Ownership (compulsory) + either Enterprise & Supplier Development or Skills Development
- 51%+ black owned QSE = automatic Level 2; 100% black owned = Level 1

### 3.4 What Level is Needed for Enterprise Contracts?

| Scenario | Minimum Level | Ideal Level |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Private enterprise (Netcare)** | Level 4 (100% recognition) | Level 1-2 (125-135% recognition) |
| **Government procurement (DOH, NHI)** | Level 1-4 (competitive points) | Level 1 (maximum preference points) |
| **Medical scheme panels** | Often no strict requirement | Level 1-2 shows transformation commitment |

**B-BBEE preference points in government procurement:**
- Contracts up to R1 million: **80/20 system** (20 points for B-BBEE)
- Contracts above R1 million: **90/10 system** (10 points for B-BBEE)
- Level 1 gets maximum points in both systems

**Sources**: [CIPC B-BBEE certificates](https://www.cipc.co.za/?page_id=3612) | [Free CIPC certificate guide](https://www.digitalstreetsa.com/free-b-bbee-certificate-from-cipc-in-2025-complete-online-application-guide/) | [B-BBEE for SMEs](https://chamberlink.co.za/understanding-b-bbee-for-south-african-smes-a-simple-practical-guide/) | [EME/QSE classifications](https://beeratings.com/emes-qses-and-start-up-enterprises/) | [B-BBEE Commission FAQ](https://www.bbbeecommission.co.za/faqs/)

## 4. POPIA Compliance for Vendors

### 4.1 Vendor Classification Under POPIA

As a vendor processing Netcare patient data, Visio Health OS is an **Operator** (data processor). Netcare is the **Responsible Party** (data controller).

| Role | Entity | Obligations |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Responsible Party** | Netcare | Determines purpose and means of processing; accountable for compliance |
| **Operator** | Visio Health OS (Touchline Agency) | Processes data only per Responsible Party's instructions; must implement own security measures |

### 4.2 Health Data — Special Personal Information

Under POPIA Section 26, health information is **Special Personal Information**. Processing is **prohibited by default** unless:

- The data subject (patient) gives **explicit consent**, OR
- Processing is **necessary for medical purposes** by a health care provider subject to a confidentiality obligation, OR
- Processing is required by **law** (e.g., medical scheme claims)

As an Operator, you must ensure your platform enables Netcare to meet these conditions (consent management, purpose limitation, access controls).

**New Regulations (February 2026)**: The *Regulations relating to the Processing of Data Subjects' Health Information by Certain Responsible Parties, 2026* were signed by Chairperson Adv Pansy Tlakula on 27 February 2026. These largely mirror existing POPIA framework but reinforce obligations. There is **no grace period** — compliance is immediate.

**Sources**: [POPIA health data regulations 2026](https://itlawco.com/popia-health-data-regulations-2026/) | [Bizcommunity: new PoPIA health regulations](https://www.bizcommunity.com/article/new-popia-regulations-on-health-data-protection-what-you-need-to-know-177076a)

### 4.3 Information Officer Registration

**Yes, you must register an Information Officer with the Information Regulator.**

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Who must register** | All private bodies (including Pty Ltd companies) that process personal information |
| **Default IO** | If no one is designated, the **head of the organization** (CEO/sole director) is deemed the Information Officer |
| **Registration portal** | Information Regulator E-Services Portal ([https://eservices.inforegulator.org.za](https://eservices.inforegulator.org.za)) |
| **Information needed** | IO personal details, Deputy IO details (optional but recommended), company details, SIC codes, processing purposes |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Timeline** | Immediate upon form submission |
| **Consequence of non-registration** | IO duties still apply to the head of the organization; potential enforcement action |

**Sources**: [Information Officer registration](https://www.michalsons.com/focus-areas/privacy-and-data-protection/information-officer-popi-paia) | [Bowmans: IO registration guide](https://bowmanslaw.com/insights/popia-what-you-need-to-know-about-the-appointment-and-registration-of-information-officers/) | [GoLegal: Registration process](https://www.golegal.co.za/information-regulator-registration/)

### 4.4 PAIA Manual (Section 51)

**Yes, you must publish a PAIA manual.**

Every private body must compile and publish a manual under Section 51 of the Promotion of Access to Information Act (PAIA). The deadline was 31 December 2021.

**Required contents:**
1. Contact details of the head of the private body and Information Officer
2. Description of the Section 10 Guide on how to use PAIA
3. Records available without a request
4. Records available under other legislation
5. Description of subjects on which the body holds records
6. **POPIA-specific sections**: Purpose of processing, categories of personal information processed, planned transborder flows, description of information security measures

**Where to publish**: On your website + a physical copy at your principal place of business

**Template**: The Information Regulator provides a free template at [https://inforegulator.org.za](https://inforegulator.org.za/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/PAIA-Manual-Template-Private-Body.pdf)

**Note**: HealthOps already has a PAIA manual framework — adapt it for Touchline Agency / Visio Health OS.

**Sources**: [Section 51 Manual guide](https://accesstoinformation.co.za/PAIA/section-51-manual/) | [Information Regulator template](https://inforegulator.org.za/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/PAIA-Manual-Template-Private-Body.pdf)

### 4.5 Operator Agreement Essentials

The Operator Agreement (DPA) must cover:

1. **Processing instructions**: Only process per Netcare's documented instructions
2. **Security safeguards**: Encryption (AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit), access controls, audit logging, MFA
3. **Confidentiality**: All staff with access must be bound by confidentiality obligations
4. **Sub-processing**: List sub-processors; obtain prior written consent before adding new ones
5. **Data subject rights**: Assist Netcare in fulfilling access, correction, deletion requests
6. **Breach notification**: Notify Netcare **immediately** upon discovering a compromise
7. **Data return/deletion**: Return data in agreed format upon contract termination; certify destruction
8. **Audit and inspection**: Allow Netcare (or their auditors) to inspect your data processing operations
9. **Cross-border transfers**: Disclose and justify any international data transfers

### 4.6 Breach Notification Obligations as Operator

| Step | Action | Timeframe |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Operator discovers potential breach | — |
| 2 | Operator notifies Responsible Party (Netcare) | **Immediately** (POPIA Section 21(2)) |
| 3 | Responsible Party assesses if compromise occurred | As quickly as possible |
| 4 | Responsible Party notifies Information Regulator | **As soon as reasonably possible** (POPIA says this; Regulator guidance says **72 hours**) |
| 5 | Responsible Party notifies affected data subjects | **As soon as reasonably possible** after notifying Regulator |
| 6 | Notification must include: nature of breach, estimated scope, measures taken, recommendations for affected individuals | In writing |

**Key point**: The notification obligation falls on the **Responsible Party** (Netcare), but as Operator you have a duty to notify them immediately. Build this into your incident response plan and runbook.

**Sources**: [POPIA breach notification](https://meyerattorneys.co.za/2025/09/03/popia-breach-notification/) | [Baker McKenzie: SA breach notification](https://resourcehub.bakermckenzie.com/en/resources/global-data-and-cyber-handbook/emea/south-africa/topics/security-requirements-and-breach-notification) | [Polity: Data breach responsibilities](https://www.polity.org.za/article/data-breach-reporting-responsibilities-and-penalties-in-south-africa-what-businesses-need-to-know-2026-01-28)

## 5. Revenue Models for Healthcare SaaS in SA

### 5.1 How SA Health Tech Vendors Charge

| Model | Description | Used By |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Per-user/per-month** | Fixed monthly fee per practitioner or admin user | GoodX, most SaaS platforms |
| **Per-clinic/per-month** | Flat fee per facility with unlimited users | Common for enterprise deals |
| **Percentage of claims** | % of monthly claim value processed through the platform | Healthbridge |
| **Per-transaction** | Fee per claim submitted, per appointment, per SMS sent | Claims switching houses |
| **Tiered subscription** | Feature-based tiers (Basic/Pro/Enterprise) | GoodX, international SaaS |
| **License + support** | Upfront license fee + annual maintenance/support (legacy model) | Elixir (CDT Medical), older systems |

### 5.2 Competitor Pricing Benchmarks

| Vendor | Model | Estimated Pricing | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------------------|-------|
| **GoodX** | Per-user/month, tiered | Core: R200/mo, Plus: R650/mo, Premium: R995/mo, Medical: R1,495/mo (up to 3 users) | Additional practitioners R65-R500/mo; quote-based for enterprise |
| **Healthbridge** | % of claim value | Not publicly disclosed; varies by specialty and claim volume | No long-term contracts; 1-month notice termination |
| **Elixir Live** (CDT Medical) | License + monthly | Not publicly disclosed; quote-based by specialty and modules | Focus on billing and claims switching |
| **Pabau** | Per-user/month | R500-R3,000+/user/month (international pricing, SA range varies) | Clinic management focused |
| **International EHR** (Epic, athena) | Enterprise licensing | R50,000-R500,000+/month for hospital-scale | Not directly comparable |

### 5.4 White-Label Premium

### 5.5 Centralized vs Per-Clinic Billing

**Recommendation: Bill centrally to Netcare head office (Sandton)**

- Netcare Medicross is managed centrally, not as independent franchises
- One invoice, one payment, one procurement relationship
- Simplifies contract management and reduces admin
- Individual clinic metrics tracked in the platform for operational reporting

## 6. Banking and Payment Setup

### 6.1 Banking Requirements for Enterprise SaaS

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Business bank account** | Must be in the company name (Touchline Agency Pty Ltd) |
| **Account type** | Business cheque/current account (not savings) |
| **Bank** | Big 4 preferred (FNB, Standard Bank, Absa, Nedbank) for enterprise credibility |
| **Bank confirmation letter** | Required for Netcare supplier registration (not older than 3 months) |
| **FICA compliance** | Company must be FICA-verified at the bank |
| **Online banking** | EFT payment capability; Netcare likely pays via EFT |
| **Dedicated account** | Consider a separate account for healthcare revenue for clean reporting |

### 6.2 Can Touchline Agency Receive Payments Directly?

**Yes**, provided:
- The company is registered with CIPC
- Has a valid tax clearance certificate
- Is VAT-registered (mandatory once turnover exceeds R1M; voluntary below R1M)
- Has a FICA-compliant business bank account
- Company details match across all registration documents (CIPC, SARS, bank)

### 6.3 Separate Entity for Healthcare?

**Not required immediately, but recommended for scale:**

| Scenario | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Initial contract (Year 1)** | Use Touchline Agency Pty Ltd — simplest path; avoid delays |
| **Revenue exceeds R5M/year** | Consider registering a dedicated healthcare entity (e.g., "Visio Health Technologies Pty Ltd") |
| **Multiple healthcare clients** | Dedicated entity provides cleaner liability separation and sector credibility |
| **Investor readiness** | Investors prefer clean, single-purpose entities |

### 6.4 Escrow Arrangements

Escrow is uncommon for SaaS subscription contracts in SA. It may be relevant for:
- **Source code escrow**: Netcare may request a software escrow agreement (NCC Group or Iron Mountain) ensuring access to source code if the vendor ceases trading
- **Implementation payments**: Large upfront implementation fees may use milestone-based payment rather than escrow
- **Typical escrow cost**: R15,000-R30,000/year for source code escrow

### 6.5 Foreign Exchange Considerations

| Cost Component | Currency | Mitigation |
|----------------|----------|------------|
| **Anthropic API** (Claude) | USD | Budget at current rate + 15% buffer; consider annual prepay |
| **Vercel hosting** | USD | Predictable monthly cost; budget accordingly |
| **Supabase** | USD | Predictable; consider self-hosted Supabase for cost control |
| **Google AI** (Gemini) | USD | Pay-per-use; budget per transaction volume |

**Recommendation**: Price your SA contracts in **ZAR** with a **quarterly FX adjustment clause** or build a 20% buffer into pricing to absorb Rand volatility.

## 8. Implementation Planning Template

### 8.1 The 48-Hour Facility Deployment Model

This is a competitive differentiator — most legacy vendors take 2-6 months for implementation. The 48-hour model works for Medicross-scale clinics (not full hospitals).

#### Hour 0-8: Pre-Deployment (Day Before)

| Task | Owner | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| Confirm internet connectivity at clinic (minimum 10 Mbps) | Client IT + Visio | 30 min |
| Provision clinic instance in Visio Health OS | Visio DevOps | 1 hour |
| Configure white-label branding (logo, colors, domain) | Visio | 2 hours |
| Create admin accounts for clinic manager and lead practitioner | Visio | 30 min |
| Pre-load medical scheme contracts and tariff tables | Visio | 2 hours |
| Configure appointment templates (per specialty) | Visio | 1 hour |
| Load practitioner profiles and HPCSA numbers | Client + Visio | 1 hour |

#### Hour 8-24: Data Migration (Day 1)

| Task | Owner | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| Export patient demographics from legacy system (CSV/HL7) | Client IT | 2 hours |
| Import patient demographics into Visio Health OS | Visio | 2 hours |
| Validate data integrity (record counts, field completeness) | Visio QA | 2 hours |
| Import appointment history (last 12 months) | Visio | 2 hours |
| Import outstanding claims and payment records | Visio | 3 hours |
| Configure medical aid scheme integrations | Visio | 3 hours |
| Parallel-run test: submit 10 test claims through new system | Visio + Client | 2 hours |

#### Hour 24-40: Training (Day 2)

| Task | Owner | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| Reception staff training: appointments, check-in, payments | Visio Trainer | 3 hours |
| Practitioner training: clinical notes, prescriptions, referrals | Visio Trainer | 3 hours |
| Practice manager training: billing, claims, reports, analytics | Visio Trainer | 3 hours |
| AI features demo: triage agent, claims pre-validation, auto-coding | Visio Trainer | 2 hours |
| Q&A and hands-on practice session | Visio Trainer | 2 hours |
| Configure user roles and permissions | Visio + Client | 1 hour |
| Print quick-reference cards for each workstation | Visio | 1 hour |

#### Hour 40-48: Go-Live (Day 2 Evening / Day 3 Morning)

| Task | Owner | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| Switch DNS / enable production URL | Visio DevOps | 30 min |
| First live patient registration | Client staff | 15 min |
| First live claim submission | Client staff | 15 min |
| Monitor real-time error dashboard | Visio | Continuous |
| On-site support for first 4 hours of live operation | Visio | 4 hours |
| Sign-off: clinic manager confirms go-live acceptance | Client | 15 min |

### 8.2 Client Staff Requirements

| Role | Required From Client | When |
|------|---------------------|------|
| **IT contact** | 1 person with admin access to legacy system + network | Pre-deployment + Day 1 |
| **Clinic manager** | Decision maker for configuration choices | All 48 hours |
| **Lead practitioner** | For clinical workflow validation | Day 2 (training) |
| **Reception staff** | All reception staff for training | Day 2 (training) |
| **Practice manager / billing** | For billing workflow and claims setup | Day 1-2 |

### 8.3 Post-Deployment Support

| Period | Support Level | Detail |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Week 1** | Dedicated on-call support | Named support engineer, 08:00-17:00 SAST, 15-min response |
| **Week 2-4** | Priority support | Dedicated Slack/Teams channel, 1-hour response |
| **Month 2-3** | Standard support + weekly check-in | Scheduled calls, ticket-based support |
| **Month 4+** | BAU support per SLA | Standard SLA terms apply |

### 8.4 Handling Zycus Procurement for Formal Contracting

The 48-hour deployment model sits **within** the formal procurement process, not outside it:

1. **Before deployment**: Complete Zycus registration, accreditation, and MSA signing
2. **Pilot agreement**: Negotiate a 3-6 month pilot covering 3-5 clinics under a simplified SOW (Statement of Work) rather than full MSA — faster procurement approval
3. **Full rollout**: After pilot success, execute full MSA through Zycus iContract for remaining clinics
4. **Purchase orders**: Netcare likely issues POs through Zycus iSource; ensure your invoicing matches PO numbers exactly
