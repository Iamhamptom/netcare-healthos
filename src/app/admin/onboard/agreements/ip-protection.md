**WHEREAS:**

A. VisioHealth has developed and owns proprietary healthcare technology comprising software, artificial intelligence models, machine learning protocols, data architectures, and related intellectual property;

## 1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1. Unless the context indicates otherwise, capitalised terms not defined herein shall have the meanings ascribed to them in the NDA (VH-NDA-2026-001) and MoU (VH-MOU-2026-001). In addition:

1.1.1. **"Background IP"** means all Intellectual Property owned by or licensed to a Party prior to the Commencement Date or developed by a Party independently of the collaboration contemplated herein;

1.1.2. **"Clinical IP"** means Dr. Lamola's pre-existing and independently developed treatment protocols, clinical methodologies, patient relationships, referral networks, professional reputation and goodwill, and medical know-how;

1.1.3. **"Contribution"** means any idea, suggestion, feedback, clinical workflow, feature request, data input, domain expertise, or other input provided by Dr. Lamola in connection with or relating to the Platform;

1.1.4. **"Foreground IP"** means all Intellectual Property created, developed, or conceived in the course of or arising from the collaboration between the Parties;

1.1.5. **"Platform IP"** means all Intellectual Property in and to the Platform, as more fully described in clause 3;

1.1.6. **"Work Product"** means any software, code, documentation, designs, models, data structures, algorithms, processes, or other deliverables created in connection with the collaboration.

## 2. VISIOHEALTH PLATFORM IP — SCHEDULE OF PROPRIETARY ASSETS

2.1. The Parties acknowledge and agree that the following constitute proprietary assets of VisioHealth, each of which represents significant investment in research, development, and innovation:

2.1.1. **UIS Sovereign Data Architecture**
   - Description: An exclusive partnership with UIS for their technology stack, enabling sovereign data processing and storage within South African borders;
   - Characteristics: Exclusive licensing arrangement; custom integration layer; data residency enforcement architecture; sovereign cloud orchestration;
   - Status: Proprietary, exclusive to VisioHealth;

2.1.2. **Local AI Model Fine-Tuning for South African Healthcare Inference**
   - Description: Methodologies, processes, and infrastructure for fine-tuning large language models and specialist AI models to perform inference on South African healthcare data, within South African data sovereignty requirements;
   - Characteristics: Custom training pipelines; SA healthcare-specific datasets; localised medical terminology models; on-premise and sovereign cloud inference architecture;
   - Status: Proprietary, trade secret;

2.1.3. **Machine Learning Protocols for ICD-10 SA Coding**
   - Description: Proprietary machine learning models and protocols for automated International Classification of Diseases, 10th Revision, South African Adaptation (ICD-10 SA) coding, including diagnosis suggestion, procedure mapping, and medical aid tariff alignment;
   - Characteristics: Custom-trained classification models; SA-specific code mappings; medical aid scheme integration; continuous learning feedback loops;
   - Status: Proprietary, patentable;

2.1.4. **WhatsApp AI Agent Healthcare System**
   - Description: An integrated AI agent system operating within the WhatsApp Business API for automated patient communication, appointment scheduling, prescription reminders, follow-up management, triage assessment, and clinical workflow support;
   - Characteristics: Natural language processing in multiple South African languages; POPIA-compliant patient interaction; automated escalation protocols; integration with practice management system;
   - Status: Proprietary, patentable;

2.1.5. **Multi-Tenant White-Label Practice Management Architecture**
   - Description: A software architecture enabling multiple independent medical practices to operate on a single platform instance with complete data isolation, custom branding, individual domains, and practice-specific AI agent configuration;
   - Characteristics: Tenant isolation at database level; per-practice AI model configuration; custom branding engine; subscription tier management; role-based access control;
   - Status: Proprietary, trade secret;

2.1.6. **POPIA-Compliant AI Data Processing Pipeline**
   - Description: An end-to-end data processing pipeline that ensures all AI operations (training, inference, analytics) comply with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013, including consent management, data minimisation, purpose limitation, and audit logging;
   - Characteristics: Automated consent tracking; data anonymisation layers; audit trail generation; Information Regulator reporting capabilities; cross-border transfer controls;
   - Status: Proprietary, regulatory compliance advantage;

2.1.7. **Credit-Based AI Usage Metering System**
   - Description: A proprietary system for metering, billing, and managing AI resource consumption across multiple tenants, including per-query cost attribution, credit allocation, usage analytics, and dynamic pricing;
   - Characteristics: Real-time usage tracking; credit pool management; overage handling; usage-based billing integration; cost optimisation algorithms;
   - Status: Proprietary;

2.1.8. **GP-to-Specialist Referral Pipeline with AI Triage**
   - Description: An AI-powered system for managing and optimising patient referrals from general practitioners to specialists, incorporating automated triage assessment, specialist matching, appointment coordination, and outcome tracking;
   - Characteristics: AI triage classification; specialist directory integration; referral urgency scoring; outcome feedback loops; referral analytics;
   - Status: Proprietary, patentable;

2.1.9. **Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Protocols for Healthcare Billing**
   - Description: Proprietary protocols integrated into the healthcare billing system for detecting and preventing fraudulent billing patterns, including unusual claim patterns, duplicate billing, upcoding detection, and suspicious transaction flagging;
   - Characteristics: Pattern recognition algorithms; anomaly detection; regulatory reporting integration; real-time monitoring; risk scoring;
   - Status: Proprietary, regulatory compliance advantage.

## 3. OWNERSHIP OF PLATFORM IP

3.1. **VisioHealth Ownership.** All Platform IP described in clause 2, and all improvements, modifications, enhancements, and derivative works thereto, shall be and remain the sole and exclusive property of VisioHealth, regardless of the source of any idea, suggestion, or input that contributed to their creation or improvement.

3.2. **Confirmation of Ownership.** Dr. Lamola acknowledges that:

3.2.1. VisioHealth has invested substantial time, effort, and financial resources in developing the Platform IP;

3.2.2. the Platform IP constitutes valuable trade secrets and proprietary know-how of VisioHealth;

3.2.3. Dr. Lamola has no ownership interest in the Platform IP by virtue of this Agreement, the subscription relationship, or any advisory role, unless otherwise expressly agreed in a separate written agreement.

---

## 4. DR. LAMOLA'S CLINICAL IP

4.1. **Retained Ownership.** Dr. Lamola shall at all times retain sole and exclusive ownership of:

4.1.1. all Clinical IP existing prior to the Commencement Date;

4.1.2. treatment protocols, clinical methodologies, and medical know-how developed independently by Dr. Lamola outside the scope of the collaboration;

4.1.3. patient relationships, referral networks, and professional goodwill;

4.1.4. Dr. Lamola's professional qualifications, certifications, and practising rights;

4.1.5. patient records and clinical data (which remain subject to POPIA, the National Health Act 61 of 2003, and the Health Professions Council of South Africa ethical rules).

4.2. **Licence to VisioHealth.** To the extent that Dr. Lamola's Clinical IP is incorporated into the Platform (e.g., clinical workflow templates, triage protocols, or specialty-specific configurations), Dr. Lamola grants VisioHealth a non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable licence to use, reproduce, modify, and sublicense such Clinical IP solely within the Platform, provided that:

4.2.1. the Clinical IP is used in a generalised, anonymised form;

4.2.2. no patient-identifying information is included;

4.2.3. attribution to Dr. Lamola is provided where commercially reasonable.

## 5. ASSIGNMENT OF CONTRIBUTIONS

5.1. **Assignment.** Dr. Lamola hereby irrevocably assigns to VisioHealth all right, title, and interest (including all Intellectual Property rights) in and to any Contribution made by Dr. Lamola in connection with or relating to the Platform, with effect from the date on which each such Contribution is made.

5.2. **Scope of Assignment.** The assignment in clause 5.1 includes, without limitation:

5.2.1. feature requests and product suggestions;

5.2.2. clinical workflow designs created specifically for the Platform;

5.2.3. user interface and user experience feedback;

5.2.4. AI model training feedback and corrections;

5.2.5. integration ideas and interoperability suggestions;

5.2.6. any software, documentation, or other Work Product created by or for Dr. Lamola specifically for incorporation into the Platform.

5.3. **Further Assurances.** Dr. Lamola agrees to execute all documents and take all steps reasonably requested by VisioHealth to perfect, register, or enforce the rights assigned under this clause, including but not limited to patent applications, design registrations, and copyright assignments.

5.4. **Power of Attorney.** Dr. Lamola hereby irrevocably appoints VisioHealth as his attorney-in-fact for the sole purpose of executing documents and taking actions to perfect, register, or enforce the intellectual property rights assigned under this clause, in the event that Dr. Lamola is unable or unwilling to do so. This appointment is coupled with an interest and shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

5.5. **Moral Rights.** To the extent permitted by law, Dr. Lamola waives any moral rights (or similar rights under any jurisdiction) in respect of any Contribution or Work Product assigned to VisioHealth.

## 6. BACKGROUND IP

6.1. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as transferring ownership of either Party's Background IP to the other Party.

6.2. To the extent that a Party's Background IP is necessarily required for the other Party to exercise its rights under this Agreement, the owning Party grants the other Party a non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free licence to use such Background IP solely for the purposes contemplated in this Agreement.

7.1.2. the AI triage system for GP-to-specialist referrals (method and system);

7.1.4. the ICD-10 SA machine learning coding system (method and system);

7.1.5. the AML detection protocols for healthcare billing (method and system);

7.1.6. the credit-based AI usage metering system (method and system).

7.2. All patent applications in respect of the inventions described in clause 7.1 shall be filed in the name of VisioHealth. Dr. Lamola agrees to provide all reasonable assistance in connection with such patent applications and to execute all necessary documents.

7.3. If Dr. Lamola is named as an inventor on any patent application, he shall be entitled to recognition as an inventor but shall have no ownership interest in the patent unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing.

## 8. RESTRICTIVE COVENANT

8.1. Dr. Lamola undertakes that during the term of this Agreement and for a period of twenty-four (24) months following its termination, he shall not, directly or indirectly:

8.1.1. develop, create, or contribute to any healthcare technology platform that competes with the Platform;

8.1.2. assist any third party in developing, creating, or marketing any healthcare technology platform that competes with the Platform;

8.1.3. disclose any Platform IP to any competitor of VisioHealth;

8.1.4. solicit, entice, or encourage any employee, contractor, or consultant of VisioHealth to leave the employment or service of VisioHealth.

8.2. The restrictions in clause 8.1 are limited to the healthcare technology sector in the Republic of South Africa and the Parties agree that such restrictions are reasonable and necessary for the protection of VisioHealth's legitimate business interests.

8.3. If any restriction in clause 8.1 is found by a court of competent jurisdiction to be unreasonable or unenforceable, the Parties agree that such restriction shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it reasonable and enforceable.

## 11. DURATION AND TERMINATION

11.1. This Agreement shall take effect on the Commencement Date and shall remain in force for the duration of the commercial relationship between the Parties, unless terminated earlier by:

11.1.1. mutual written agreement;

11.1.2. either Party, upon sixty (60) days' written notice to the other Party;

11.1.3. either Party, immediately upon written notice, in the event of a material breach by the other Party that remains unremedied for thirty (30) Business Days following written notice of such breach.

11.2. Notwithstanding termination:

11.2.1. all IP assignments effected under clause 5 shall be and remain irrevocable;

11.2.2. the licence granted under clause 4.2 shall survive termination;

11.2.3. the restrictive covenants in clause 8 shall survive for the period specified therein;

11.2.4. clauses 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 13, and 14 shall survive termination.

## 12. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION

12.1. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of South Africa.

12.2. The Parties irrevocably submit to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the High Court of South Africa, Gauteng Division, Pretoria.

## 13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

13.1. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall first be referred to the respective senior representatives of the Parties for resolution by negotiation within fifteen (15) Business Days.

13.2. If unresolved, the dispute shall be referred to mediation under the rules of the Arbitration Foundation of Southern Africa (AFSA).

13.3. If unresolved by mediation within thirty (30) Business Days, either Party may institute proceedings in terms of clause 12.2.

13.4. Either Party may seek urgent interim relief at any time.
