## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF CONFIDENTIALITY

2.1. Each Party, as Receiving Party, undertakes to the Disclosing Party:

2.1.1. to treat all Confidential Information as strictly confidential;

2.1.2. not to disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, save as expressly permitted in terms of this Agreement;

2.1.3. not to use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the evaluation and pursuit of a commercial and/or investment relationship between the Parties (the **"Permitted Purpose"**);

2.1.4. to take all reasonable steps to protect the confidentiality of the Confidential Information, which steps shall be no less rigorous than those applied by the Receiving Party to its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in any event no less than a reasonable standard of care;

2.1.5. to restrict disclosure of the Confidential Information to Authorised Representatives on a strict need-to-know basis;

2.1.6. to ensure that all Authorised Representatives to whom Confidential Information is disclosed are made aware of the confidential nature thereof and are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less restrictive than those contained in this Agreement;

2.1.7. to notify the Disclosing Party immediately in writing upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected breach of this Agreement or any unauthorised disclosure, possession, use, or knowledge of the Confidential Information;

2.1.8. not to copy, reproduce, or reduce to writing any Confidential Information except as strictly necessary for the Permitted Purpose.

2.2. The obligations in clause 2.1 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by clear and convincing evidence:

2.2.1. was in the public domain at the time of disclosure, or subsequently entered the public domain through no fault of the Receiving Party;

2.2.2. was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by the Receiving Party's written records;

2.2.3. was independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Confidential Information, as evidenced by the Receiving Party's written records;

2.2.4. was received by the Receiving Party from a third party who was lawfully entitled to disclose it without restriction; or

2.2.5. is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or order of a court or competent regulatory authority, provided that the Receiving Party:

   (a) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement prior to disclosure (to the extent legally permissible);

   (b) provides reasonable assistance to the Disclosing Party in opposing or limiting such disclosure;

   (c) discloses only the minimum amount of Confidential Information reasonably required to comply with such requirement; and

   (d) uses commercially reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for any Confidential Information so disclosed.

## 3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

3.1. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting to either Party any licence, right, title, or interest in or to the Confidential Information or any Intellectual Property of the other Party, save as may be expressly agreed in a separate written agreement.

3.2. All Confidential Information shall remain the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party.

3.3. No disclosure of Confidential Information under this Agreement shall constitute a public disclosure or otherwise prejudice the Disclosing Party's rights to obtain patent, design, trademark, copyright, or other intellectual property protection in respect of such information.

## 4. RETURN AND DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

4.1. Upon the written request of the Disclosing Party at any time, or upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement, whichever occurs first, the Receiving Party shall:

4.1.1. promptly return to the Disclosing Party all tangible materials containing or embodying Confidential Information;

4.1.2. permanently destroy all copies, reproductions, summaries, analyses, or extracts of or derived from the Confidential Information in its possession, custody, or control, including all electronic copies stored on any medium;

4.1.3. within ten (10) Business Days of such request, provide the Disclosing Party with a written certificate signed by a duly authorised representative of the Receiving Party confirming that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed in accordance with this clause.

4.2. Notwithstanding clause 4.1, the Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy of the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of determining the scope of its obligations under this Agreement, provided that such copy is kept in secure storage and remains subject to the terms of this Agreement.

5.1. All Confidential Information is provided **"as is"** and without any warranty, express or implied, as to its accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any particular purpose.

5.2. Neither Party shall be liable to the other Party for any errors or omissions in the Confidential Information or for any actions taken or not taken in reliance thereon.

5.3. Nothing in this Agreement shall oblige either Party to disclose any particular Confidential Information or to enter into any further agreement or transaction.

## 6. PENALTIES AND REMEDIES FOR BREACH

6.1. The Parties acknowledge and agree that a breach of this Agreement may cause the Disclosing Party irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone would be inadequate.

6.2. In the event of a breach or threatened breach of this Agreement, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled, without prejudice to any other rights or remedies available to it at law or in equity, to:

6.2.1. seek urgent interim and/or final interdictory relief from any court of competent jurisdiction in the Republic of South Africa, without the requirement of proving actual damages;

6.2.2. claim damages, including but not limited to direct, indirect, and consequential damages suffered as a result of the breach;

6.2.3. claim a contractual penalty in the amount of **Five Hundred Thousand South African Rand (R500,000.00)** for each breach of this Agreement, which amount the Parties agree constitutes a genuine pre-estimate of the damages likely to be suffered by the Disclosing Party as a result of a breach, without prejudice to the Disclosing Party's right to claim actual damages exceeding such amount in terms of the Conventional Penalties Act 15 of 1962.

6.3. The Receiving Party shall indemnify and hold harmless the Disclosing Party against all losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including legal costs on an attorney-and-own-client scale) arising from or in connection with any breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party or its Authorised Representatives.

## 7. DURATION AND TERMINATION

7.1. This Agreement shall take effect on the Commencement Date and shall remain in force for a period of **five (5) years** from the Commencement Date (the **"Term"**), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this clause.

7.2. Either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving the other Party not less than thirty (30) days' prior written notice.

7.3. Notwithstanding the expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

7.3.1. the obligations of confidentiality in respect of Confidential Information disclosed during the Term shall continue for a period of five (5) years from the date of expiration or termination;

7.3.2. the obligations in respect of trade secrets shall continue indefinitely for so long as such information remains a trade secret;

7.3.3. clauses 3, 4, 6, 7.3, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13 shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## 8. DATA PROTECTION

8.1. To the extent that any Confidential Information constitutes "personal information" as defined in POPIA, the Parties agree to process such information in compliance with POPIA and any other applicable data protection legislation.

8.2. Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect any personal information received from the other Party against loss, damage, unauthorised access, or unlawful processing.

8.3. Neither Party shall transfer any personal information received from the other Party to any jurisdiction outside the Republic of South Africa without the prior written consent of the other Party and in compliance with section 72 of POPIA.
