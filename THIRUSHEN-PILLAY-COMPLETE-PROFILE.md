### 2. Claims Rejection and Cash Flow Impact
- Claims rejections directly delay cash inflow
- SA medical schemes are slow payers and aggressive rejectors
- Each rejected claim requires manual investigation and resubmission
- **Cost of a rejection**: Staff time + delayed cash + potential write-off

### 3. Manual Processes and Excel Hell
- Despite SAP at group level, many primary care processes are still manual
- Clinic-level reporting likely involves spreadsheet consolidation
- Budget vs. actual analysis across 88 clinics is labour-intensive
- **The FD knows this pain**: He already moved Afrocentric from Excel to Oracle

### 4. Fragmented Systems
- SAP for financial reporting
- CareOn EMR for hospitals (but Primary Care may not be fully on CareOn)
- Medicross Online Bookings (A2D24)
- Clicks pharmacy system (separate, outsourced)
- Occupational health tracking (separate)
- Prime Cure managed care system (separate)
- **Result**: No single source of truth for divisional performance

### 5. Debtor Management Across 30+ Medical Schemes
- Each scheme has different payment terms, tariff structures, and rejection reasons
- Aging management is complex — some schemes pay in 14 days, others in 90
- Need for scheme-specific collection strategies
- Write-off decisions affect P&L and require governance

### 6. NHI Uncertainty
- NHI Act signed into law, Phase 1 (2023-2026) underway, Phase 2 (2026-2028)
- Impact on private primary care is existential — will Medicross clinics become NHI providers?
- Tariff uncertainty — will NHI rates cover costs?
- **Planning nightmare**: Cannot forecast 3-5 years with NHI overhang

### 7. Regulatory Burden
- **POPIA**: Patient data protection — any breach has financial and reputational consequences
- **CMS**: Council for Medical Schemes compliance for managed care (Prime Cure)
- **HPCSA**: Health Professions Council billing compliance
- **OHSA**: Occupational Health and Safety Act for occupational health services
- **Companies Act**: Director duties and financial reporting obligations
- **JSE Listings Requirements**: As part of a listed group
- **IFRS**: International Financial Reporting Standards
- **King IV**: Corporate governance requirements

### 8. Cost Inflation vs. Tariff Growth
- Medical scheme tariffs typically increase by CPI or below
- Staff costs (nurses, admin) increase above CPI
- Medical consumables inflation is global
- Electricity costs (load shedding backup) are significant
- **Margin squeeze**: Costs rise faster than revenue per consultation

### 9. Practitioner Retention and Productivity
- 568 independent practitioners — they can leave for competitors
- Revenue per practitioner must justify the infrastructure cost
- Locum costs when practitioners are unavailable
- Work-life balance pressures post-COVID affecting practitioner availability

### 10. Capital Allocation in a Declining Revenue Environment
- 88 clinics need ongoing maintenance and equipment replacement
- Limited capex budget when revenue is declining
- Must prioritise highest-ROI investments
- Digital transformation competes with physical infrastructure needs
